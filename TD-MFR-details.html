Numerically Constructed Titov–Démoulin (TD) magnetic flux rope model

I have successfully constructed the Titov–Démoulin (TD) magnetic flux rope model, which is a widely used analytical model for representing twisted magnetic structures in solar active regions and serving as an initial condition for MHD simulations. I built the TD geometry by placing two magnetic charges and adding a ring current and line current to create a realistic coronal magnetic field. I then compute the magnetic field by combining contributions from the charges, ring current, and toroidal field, allowing me to study the full 3D magnetic configuration. Using this framework, I tested when the system remains in force-free equilibrium and when it becomes unstable.

This model serves as a framework to study magnetic stability and eruption mechanisms in solar active regions.
Additionally, I have calculated and visualized the decay index of the overlying bipolar magnetic field and examined how it decreases with height. From the visualization, it is clear that the bipolar magnetic field strength decreases with height, as expected. Additionally, the twisted flux rope does not exceed the critical decay index value of 1.5, which suggests that the configuration is stable and does not undergo torus instability.
